FAQQuestions

What is Zakat?
Zakat is an obligatory annual levy of 2.5% on zakatable wealth (cash, gold, investments) held for a lunar year, due when the wealth exceeds the nisab threshold (about 85g of gold). Islamic banks help compute, collect and distribute it.
What business area does Zakat Distribution belong to?
Zakat Distribution sits in the Shariah Governance and Compliance business area — the mandatory Shariah-governance layer that supervises, audits and evidences compliance across every Islamic banking line. It follows the BIAN Distribute functional pattern over the Zakat Disbursement asset, with the Zakat Disbursement Distribution as its control record.
How is this isolated from conventional banking?
Completely. The Islamic landscape shares no code, repositories, namespaces or events with the conventional platform. This domain lives in the isb-governance namespace with an isb-* repository and com.bank.islamic package, so the two can never mix by accident.
Is this a certified Shariah-compliant product?
No. It is an educational reference implementation in the BIAN service-domain style. Actual Shariah compliance of any real deployment requires certification by a qualified Shariah Supervisory Board. Standards referenced: AAOIFI, IFSB, IIFM.
